# Current, Voltage, and Resistance

Electric current is the rate of flow of electric charge through a conductor. It tells us how much charge is moving through the circuit in a given time.

Voltage, also called potential difference, is the electric push that makes charges move. Without a difference in potential, current will not flow.

Resistance is the opposition offered by a material to the flow of current. A higher resistance reduces the current for the same voltage.

## Ohm's Law

Ohm's law says that current is directly proportional to voltage and inversely proportional to resistance, as long as temperature remains constant.

Formula: V = I x R

Where:
- V is voltage
- I is current
- R is resistance

## Simple Understanding

If the push becomes stronger, more charge flows. If the path becomes harder, less charge flows. So voltage is the push, current is the flow, and resistance is the difficulty in the path.

## Common Confusion

Students often confuse voltage with current because both are related to electricity. But they are not the same. Voltage causes current. Current is the movement that happens because of that cause.
